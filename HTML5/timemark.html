Time

The time element contains both a human readable part between the tags and a machine readable part in the datetime attribute. While the spec initially only supported precise dates, now fuzzy dates can be defined.

Furthermore..

Local times can be defined by appending a "Z" to the timezone: <time datetime="09:00Z"> is 9am, UTC.

Timezone offset may be indicated by a "+" or a "-" as in: <time datetime="09:00-5">.

Durations

In HTML5 <time> you can represent durations with the prefix "P" for period. Attributes are: "D" for days, "H" for hours and "S" for seconds.

Thus <time datetime="P4D"> indicates a duration of 4 days. Further precision can be achieved by using a "T" after the "P" as in <time datetime="PT23H 9M 2.343S"> which amounts to a duration of 23 hours, 9 minutes, and 2.343 seconds.
